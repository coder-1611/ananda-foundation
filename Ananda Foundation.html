Our Story

Grounded in proximity to community

Ananda Foundation was founded by Anand Deo after years working at the UN Office for Human Rights in Geneva and on the ground with Medic Mobile and the Grameen Foundation. He came home to Bihar in 2019 with one conviction: lasting change only comes from sustained presence, not short project cycles from the outside.

Working in Sikariya and surrounding villages in Nawada, we live alongside tribal and indigenous communities — running medical camps, distributing menstrual health supplies to schoolgirls, training women in beekeeping and organic farming, and now building the region's first community-owned pad manufacturing unit.

"Change begins when you stay close to people's realities — and allow their needs to guide your work." — Anand Deo, Founder · Ananda Foundation, Mumbai

Active Campaign · 2026

Sanitary Pad Manufacturing Unit — Sikariya

We are raising €8,000 to install a pad manufacturing unit in our own village. Zero rent, minimal overhead — and 10+ rural women employed from day one.

At full capacity, the unit will produce 80,000 affordable pads per month — both generating income for women and serving the community's health needs. Launching August 2026.
